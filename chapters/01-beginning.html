The Beginning

When a stranger became familiar

It started the way most important things do ‚Äî quietly, unremarkably, on an ordinary Tuesday afternoon in a study room I'd claimed as my own.

I didn't know his name yet. I just knew he appeared about three months ago, suddenly showing up in that small room where I spent my afternoons. He said he didn't even know that room existed before.

Twice a week. Like clockwork. First sitting across from me, then sometimes beside me, as if the distance between us was something that could be measured in chair positions.

First impression:

He would shake his head to music ‚Äî not subtly, but with this almost aggressive enthusiasm that made it impossible not to notice. I remember thinking it was a bit much. A bit childish, even.

Once, he burst out laughing at the back of the room because the charging station wasn't plugged in. Just that. Such a small thing to find so funny.

In the beginning, he never spoke first. He would sit there, do his work, and wait. Wait for me to say something. Anything. And when I did, he would listen ‚Äî really listen ‚Äî and then the conversation would unfold naturally, like he'd been waiting for permission to exist in the same space as me.

After about a month, he started initiating. Small things at first. Comments about the assignment. Questions about a concept. Always taking off his earphone on the side closer to me.

I noticed he never used contractions when texting me. Every "I am" was spelled out. Every "do not" kept its dignity. It felt formal in a way that made me feel... considered.

I don't know why that mattered. But it did.
